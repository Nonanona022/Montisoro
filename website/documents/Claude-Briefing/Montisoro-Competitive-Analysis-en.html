The market
Market context

A market that is shifting.

Long-term absence remains structurally high in Belgium and legislation is shifting towards active follow-up. That creates demand for exactly what falls between the existing players: neutral direction and structure.

01

Rising long-term absence

The number of long-term incapacitated workers in Belgium remains high, with sustained social and budgetary pressure on employers and government.

02

Stricter follow-up

Regulation pushes towards early, active follow-up and an assessment of work potential in longer incapacity — reintegration becomes an obligation, not an option.

03

Rising employer cost

With the solidarity contribution (RIT 3.0), the employer bears a growing share of the benefit cost — absence becomes visible on the income statement.

Consequence: organisations no longer seek only a medical-legal partner, but someone who directs, documents and internally anchors the whole trajectory. That is the space in which Montisoro operates.

The playing field
Four categories

Who Montisoro shares the field with.

No single player covers the whole terrain. The market breaks into four categories, each with its own strength and its own blind spot.

Category 01

External prevention services

The recognised EDPBs (incl. IDEWE, Mensura, Liantis, Securex, Cohezio). Strong in medical-legal mandate and scale. Reintegration direction is usually not in the base package.

Category 02

HR & social service providers

Payroll secretariats and HR players (incl. SD Worx, Acerta, Securex, Liantis). Strong in payroll, data and advice. Absence is adjacent, not core.

Category 03

Reintegration & career agencies

Specialised coaches, burnout and outplacement counsellors. Strong in human expertise. Often project-based and fragmented, little system or technology.

Category 04

HR-tech & wellbeing software

Absence dashboards and wellbeing platforms. Strong in tech and figures. Software without human direction or governance does not solve the complexity.

Disclaimer: named companies illustrate the category and their public positioning. This is a positioning analysis based on publicly available information (mid-2026) — not a judgement of individual providers.

The space in between

The role no one fills.

Montisoro does not compete head-on with the recognised prevention services or payroll secretariats — it connects their work. The positioning sits precisely in the space that falls between the categories.

01

Neutral director

Independent of payroll, insurer or medical service — Montisoro steers the trajectory and keeps all parties connected.

02

One system, four layers

Direction, governance, capability and technology together — where competitors cover one layer.

03

Capability that stays

Success measured in independence, not dependence. Knowledge stays in the organisation.

04

Proprietary technology

Storm (since 2023) and Casey AI give a tech edge that pure service providers do not have.

Montisoro does not replace the occupational physician. It fills the missing director role — and makes it scalable.

Sources & method

What this is based on.

This analysis was compiled from publicly available market information and the regulatory framework around well-being at work in Belgium, as of mid-2026.

Context consulted
  • The Belgian framework for external services for prevention and protection at work (EDPB) and the recognised providers within it.
  • The evolution of reintegration legislation and the follow-up of long-term work incapacity.
  • The solidarity contribution for employers (RIT 3.0) and the employer cost of absence.
  • Public positioning and service offerings of the named players and categories.
Method & limitations

The analysis describes categories and their typical strengths and blind spots, not the performance of individual companies. Named companies illustrate a category. Markets and offerings evolve; this analysis is a snapshot.
