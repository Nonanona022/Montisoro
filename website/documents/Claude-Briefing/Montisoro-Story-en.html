The trigger
Where it began

19 years of commitment.
Five months of silence.

Some companies are born from an opportunity. Montisoro from a conviction.

The story does not begin with a business plan, but with a person. After nineteen years of commitment, someone dropped out — and then it stayed silent for five months. No guidance, no real landing, no role that carried the trajectory. Not from unwillingness on the organisation's part, but because that role simply did not exist.

What began as a personal experience became an observation that recurred in too many organisations. And that observation became the design principle of Montisoro.

People rarely drop out from illness alone. They also drop out on connection, safety and perspective.

The conviction
From insight to mission

Care is not a feeling.
It is a Capability.

The personal insight became a mission: care is not a feeling, but a capability you can build, structure and scale.

Montisoro builds the knowledge, structure and technology with which organisations see earlier what is breaking down, act better when it gets complex and anchor knowledge more sustainably — so that care and performance become one system.

Reintegrate What Matters — two meanings, one phrase.

Bring people back. And restore what organisations lost: connection, safety, perspective. It is at once a promise to the employee and to the organisation — because the two cannot be seen apart.

Founder & CEO. The realisation that the director role was missing became her decision to build that role herself — and to build an organisation around it.

Policy-strong, analytical and tech-savvy with grit. Laurence brings strategic insight, legal sharpness and lived experience together to make absence policy more human and more effective. From that starting point, Montisoro grew into a team of experts, case managers and engineers — and into the technology (Storm and Casey AI) that makes the approach scalable.
